Elephant

The planet’s largest terrestrial mammal with only two distinct species left in the whole world: The African elephant and the Asian elephant.

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta
  • Average Length: 20 ft
  • Average Weight: 12000 lbs
  • Average Lifespan: 60 Years
  • Habitat: forests, deserts and marshes

The word “elephant” comes from the Greek word “elephas” which means “ivory”. Baby elephant can weigh up to 260 pounds and can stand shortly after born. Elephant is considered to have largest brain compared to any other land animal. However, most of the intelligence is used in controlling its huge body.
